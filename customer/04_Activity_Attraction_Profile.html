About This Tour

Experience the infamous Alcatraz Federal Penitentiary on this comprehensive tour of "The Rock." Located 1.5 miles from San Francisco's shore, Alcatraz Island served as a federal prison from 1934 to 1963, housing notorious criminals like Al Capone and the "Birdman" Robert Stroud. Your tour begins with a scenic ferry ride across San Francisco Bay, offering spectacular views of the Golden Gate Bridge, Bay Bridge, and the city skyline. Upon arrival, knowledgeable National Park Rangers will provide an introduction to the island's rich history. Explore the cellhouse with our award-winning audio tour "Doing Time," featuring actual interviews with former inmates and correctional officers. Walk through the same cells where America's most dangerous criminals were once confined and hear their fascinating stories. Beyond its prison history, Alcatraz is also home to the West Coast's first lighthouse, early military fortifications, and natural features with unique flora and fauna. The island is a designated bird sanctuary and offers some of the best views of San Francisco.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to book in advance?

Yes, tickets often sell out weeks ahead. Booking early is recommended.

Is the tour family-friendly?

Yes, children are welcome. The ferry and island have stroller access.

What if it rains?

The tour runs rain or shine, so bring layered clothing and an umbrella.

Traveler Tips

  • Arrive 30 minutes early for ferry boarding.
  • Bring snacks since food is not sold on the island.
  • Take the earliest ferry to avoid crowds.
  • Wear comfortable shoes – lots of walking!
